# Xmip Engineering Methodology

## SACE

SACE is the architectural decision discipline used by the Xmip project.

```text
Simplify
Agree
Commit
Evolve
```

### Simplify

Reduce unnecessary complexity, clarify responsibilities and prefer durable solutions over clever or overlapping abstractions.

### Agree

Challenge assumptions until a shared architectural understanding is reached.

### Commit

Persist every agreed architectural decision in the Xmip repository before moving on. The repository is the authoritative source of truth.

### Evolve

Validate architecture through implementation, reflect on the result and improve it without losing the recorded reasoning.

## Mission

> Xmip is a project that applies SACE to create usable, durable and observable integrations.

## Engineering principle

> Simplify until responsibilities become obvious.

## SACE check

Before an architectural decision is considered complete, ask:

```text
S — Did we simplify it?
A — Do we agree?
C — Has it been committed?
E — Can it evolve through implementation without losing its purpose?
```

SACE complements delivery methods such as Scrum, Agile or SAFe. Those organize work. SACE governs architectural decisions.
